Adam Smith

Professor of Economics, Stockholm University

Adam Smith is a Professor of Economics at Stockholm University. He combines Swedish administrative register data with quasi-experimental and structural methods to study how tax and social-insurance policy shapes work, wealth, and inequality — from how people respond to marginal tax rates to the long-run effects of parental leave and the consequences of taxing wealth in an open economy. His work has appeared in the American Economic Review, the Quarterly Journal of Economics, the Journal of Political Economy, and Econometrica, and has been covered by The New York Times, the Financial Times, and The Economist.
